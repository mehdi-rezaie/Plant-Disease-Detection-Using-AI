How to identify black rot Fruit symptoms Large brown rotten areas can form anywhere on the fruit but are most common on the blossom end. Brown to black concentric rings can often be seen on larger infections. The flesh of the apple is brown but remains firm. Small, black spots can be seen on older fruit infections. These are fungal spore producing structures, called pycnidia. Some fruit mummify (shrivel and dry out) and remain attached to the tree. Occasionally fruit are infected early in the season. This results in fruit that ripen early and are rotten at the core. Branch symptoms Cankers appear as a sunken, reddish-brown area on infected branches. Cankers often have rough or cracked bark. Cankers may be hard to see.
